About Conference

The Indian Library Association (ILA) is honoured to host its 72nd Annual Conference 2027 in the historic “City of Pearls,” in collaboration with the University of Hyderabad, an Institution of Eminence. This premier international summit serves as a global nexus for librarians, information scientists, academicians, and technology innovators.

As the digital and physical frontiers of information continue to merge, this conference provides a vital forum to examine the shifting paradigms of our field. This landmark event will help redefine the role of libraries as catalysts for global knowledge and social progress.

The main theme of the conference is Future Libraries: Advancing Research & Excellence 2027 (FLARE-2027). The conference focuses on a wide range of emerging and trending topics in the field of Library and Information Science such as Libraries as Drivers of Sustainable Development, Green Libraries, Artificial Intelligence and Libraries, Assistive Technologies and Inclusive Library Services, and Emerging Technologies in the domain of Library Science.

Overall, the sub-themes of the conference encompass the majority of emerging and innovative technologies in the domain of Library and Information Science. The sessions will explore the essential contribution of libraries in anchoring the future of intellectual exchange and driving meaningful community empowerment.

About University of Hyderabad

The University of Hyderabad, established on 2 October 1974 by an Act of Parliament as a Central University, is one of India’s leading institutions for higher education and research.

Spread across a serene 2,500-acre green campus, the University comprises 12 Schools and 42 Departments offering multidisciplinary programmes in humanities, social sciences, sciences, engineering, management, fine arts, and interdisciplinary studies.

Recognized as an Institution of Eminence by the Government of India, the University is known for academic excellence, cutting-edge research, innovation, and vibrant campus life. The University has been ranked 18th among universities in the NIRF Rankings 2025 and placed in the 801–850 band in the QS World University Rankings 2025.

With over 500 teaching staff, 1,200 non-teaching staff, and around 6,000 students, the University fosters a dynamic academic and research environment supported by international collaborations, impactful publications, ongoing research projects, and patents.

Combining affordable world-class education with strong interdisciplinary learning and social commitment, the University of Hyderabad continues to be one of India’s most sought-after institutions of higher learning.

About Indira Gandhi Memorial Library

The Indira Gandhi Memorial (IGM) Library of the University of Hyderabad is a premier central library supporting the academic and research needs of faculty members, students, and research scholars.

Inaugurated on 21 October 1988 by the late Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma, then Vice-President of India, the Library is named after former Prime Minister Mrs. Indira Gandhi.

The Library houses over 4.5 lakh books, journals, theses, dissertations, and digital resources in multidisciplinary subjects, along with 24×7 air-conditioned reading facilities and access to national and international scholarly databases and e-resources.

It was the first university library in India to fully automate housekeeping operations and also partnered in the Carnegie Mellon University Million Book Project for the Universal Digital Library. Library operations are fully automated through an integrated library management system.

The Library offers advanced digital and research support services, including plagiarism detection, writing assistance, remote access, citation and indexing databases, ONOS resources, and subscriptions to 22 internationally reputed scholarly databases.

The Library also pioneered the Diploma in Library Automation and Networking (DLAN), one of the earliest professional programmes of its kind offered by a university library in India.

With modern infrastructure, digital resources, and user-centric services, the IGM Library continues to be a vibrant hub for learning, research, innovation, and scholarly excellence.

About Indian Library Association (ILA)

The Indian Library Association (ILA), established on 13 September 1933 during the All India Library Conference in Calcutta, is the premier national professional body for library and information professionals in India.

Registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, ILA has played a vital role in promoting library and information services, professional education, research, training, and advocacy for the library profession.

The Association provides a common platform for librarians, academicians, researchers, and information professionals to exchange ideas and discuss emerging trends and technological innovations through conferences, seminars, workshops, and professional meetings.

ILA also contributes to scholarly communication through its publications, including the ILA Newsletter, the Journal of Indian Library Association (JILA), and conference proceedings.

With a strong commitment to strengthening the library movement in India, ILA continues to promote professional standards, ethics, cooperation, digital transformation, and capacity building in the field of Library and Information Science.
