72nd ILA International Conference

Future Libraries: Advancing Research & Excellence (FLARE-2027)

7–9 January 2027 | University of Hyderabad

About the Conference

The Indian Library Association (ILA), in collaboration with the University of Hyderabad, is organizing the 72nd ILA International Conference on Future Libraries: Advancing Research & Excellence (FLARE-2027) from 7–9 January 2027 at the University of Hyderabad, Hyderabad, India.

FLARE-2027 aims to provide an international platform for librarians, information professionals, academicians, researchers, students, policymakers, and technology experts to exchange ideas, share experiences, and discuss the latest developments in Library and Information Science.

The conference theme highlights the importance of libraries as dynamic centers of knowledge, innovation, and lifelong learning. The conference will address contemporary issues such as Artificial Intelligence in Libraries, Open Science, Research Data Management, Digital Preservation, Smart Libraries, Assistive Technologies, and Sustainable Library Development.

Through keynote lectures, invited talks, technical sessions, panel discussions, workshops, and poster presentations, FLARE-2027 will facilitate knowledge sharing, professional networking, and collaborative partnerships among participants from India and across the world.

The conference seeks to envision the future of libraries and empower information professionals to support research, innovation, and societal development in an increasingly digital and interconnected world.

About University of Hyderabad

The University of Hyderabad, established on 2 October 1974 by an Act of Parliament, is one of India’s premier institutions of higher education and research. Situated on a sprawling 2,500-acre green campus in Hyderabad, the University comprises 12 Schools and 42 Departments offering multidisciplinary programmes in Humanities, Social Sciences, Sciences, Engineering, Management, Fine Arts, and Interdisciplinary Studies.

Recognized as an Institution of Eminence by the Government of India, the University is renowned for its academic excellence, innovative research, and vibrant campus environment. The University consistently ranks among the top higher education institutions in India and has earned national and international recognition for its contributions to teaching, research, and knowledge creation.

The University was ranked 18th among universities in the NIRF Rankings 2025 and placed in the 801–850 band in the QS World University Rankings 2025. With over 500 faculty members, 1,200 non-teaching staff, and around 6,000 students, it fosters a dynamic academic ecosystem supported by international collaborations, high-impact publications, funded research projects, and patents.

Combining world-class education, interdisciplinary learning, research excellence, innovation, and social commitment, the University of Hyderabad continues to be one of India’s most sought-after institutions of higher learning and a leading centre for knowledge creation and dissemination.

About Indian Library Association (ILA)

The Indian Library Association (ILA), established on 13 September 1933 during the All India Library Conference in Calcutta, is the premier national professional body for library and information professionals in India. Registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, ILA has played a vital role in promoting library and information services, professional education, research, training, and advocacy for the library profession.

The Association provides a common platform for librarians, academicians, researchers, and information professionals to exchange ideas and discuss emerging trends and technological innovations through conferences, seminars, workshops, and professional meetings. ILA also contributes to scholarly communication through its publications, including the ILA Newsletter, the Journal of Indian Library Association (JILA), and conference proceedings.

With a strong commitment to strengthening the library movement in India, ILA continues to promote professional standards, ethics, cooperation, digital transformation, and capacity building in the field of Library and Information Science.
